Anthropic has released Opus 4.6. At the same time, they announced that in just 14 days, using 2,000 agents, they built a C compiler.

Let that sink in.

This is the level of complexity where, if someone still says “AI can’t do that”, it’s not an opinion—it’s a sign they’re already out of sync with reality. The shift has already happened.

Since last August, I’ve been living almost non-stop in Claude Code. This isn’t theory. It’s daily practice. Thousands of hours spent on agent orchestration, agent memory, and running multi-agent systems inside highly complex, massively parallel workflows. At this point, most problems aren’t unsolvable—they’re just poorly structured.

And this is exactly what we’re now building at Keboola.

We’re turning 2,200+ hours of R&D into an operating system for business applications. Not another SaaS. Not another dashboard. But a system where AI can create, operate, and replace applications on demand.

You read about tech companies losing market value because of AI? That’s not panic. That’s realization. We’ve been selling software as static products in a world that just became dynamic.

Still clicking through browsers all day at work? An agent can already do that. No SaaS required. No vendor lock-in. Fully in-house.

All you really need is a thin glue layer—and an OS that lets AI run your applications for you.

The next few years will be wild. The future belongs to those who don’t panic when the interface disappears.
