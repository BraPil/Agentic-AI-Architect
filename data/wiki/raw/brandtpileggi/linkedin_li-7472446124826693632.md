A 30-message Claude session burns 232,000 tokens.
Most of it is Claude re-reading what you already typed.

Here is the part nobody explains:

Every message you send, Claude re-reads the entire conversation from the top.
Message 1 is cheap.
Message 30 means re-reading 29 exchanges before it even thinks about your actual question.

That is why your credits vanish by lunch.

Not the plan. The chat you refuse to close.

Here is what actually saves tokens:

- Edit your message instead of sending a follow-up. The old exchange gets replaced, not stacked.
- Fix one section, not the whole thing. "Only redo section 3, keep the rest."
- Batch tasks into one message. Three prompts equal three full reloads.
- Convert files to text before uploading. One PDF page is 1,500 to 3,000 tokens.
- New topic equals new chat. Old messages are dead weight Claude keeps re-reading.
- Match the model to the task. Sonnet and Haiku for light work, Opus for deep work.

What nobody talks about:

→ Long chats are token furnaces.
→ One developer tracked his usage and found 98.5% of tokens went to re-reading history. Only 1.5% reached the actual output.

Shorter context equals lower cost.

Every habit traces back to that one idea.
