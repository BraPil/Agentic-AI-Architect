Everyone's chasing precise, context-aware retrieval, the part of GenAI that actually delivers value.
But Agentic RAG done right depends on the full infrastructure behind it. Here are the 9 layers.

The foundation:

1. Deployment: Where agents are hosted and executed. Optimized compute, containerization, seamless scalability. Nothing runs without this layer working reliably.

2. Evaluation: Continuous testing, scoring, and feedback analysis measuring retrieval quality. How you know the system is actually working not just running.

The intelligence core:

3. LLMs: The thinking layer powering reasoning, conversation, planning, and decision-making. The brain of the operation but only one layer of nine.

4. Frameworks: Orchestration managing tool usage, memory access, prompt construction, agent routing. The connective tissue holding everything together.

The retrieval engine:

5. Vector Databases: Store dense vector representations for similarity-based search over large corpora. Where RAG actually retrieves from.

6. Embedding Models: Convert raw text into dense representations enabling similarity search and concept matching. The bridge between human language and vector space.

7. Data Extraction: Pipelines pulling structured and unstructured data from websites, documents, APIs. Garbage in, garbage out starts here. The quality of extraction caps everything downstream.

The persistence layer:

8. Memory: Persistent and contextual continuity across sessions. Enables personalization, long-term knowledge, adaptive behavior. What makes an agent feel less like a goldfish.

The trust layer:

9. Alignment and Observability: Tracks performance, monitors outputs, ensures safety, aligns behavior with expectations through validation and telemetry. The layer that makes everything else trustworthy.

Do you need all nine on day one?

No. They're a starting point, not a checklist. The skill is picking what your use case actually needs, integrating thoughtfully, and iterating continuously.

A team that bolts on all nine before having a working retrieval loop is over-engineering. A team that ignores evaluation and observability is flying blind. The art is knowing which layers your specific problem demands right now and which can wait.

Start with the retrieval core (embeddings, vector DB, LLM). Add evaluation early so you can measure. Layer in memory, observability, and data extraction as the use case grows.

Which layer does your stack handle well and which is the gap?
