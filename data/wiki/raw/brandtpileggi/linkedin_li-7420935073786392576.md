Unless you've been hiding under a rock, you've heard people going gaga about Claude Code. This week, they shipped Cowork, a more user-friendly interface, for the Mac.

I sat down for an hour over the weekend, started to FAFO, and it clicked fairly quickly.

In the last 3 days of work, I've been feeling like Bradley Cooper in Limitless. Here's what I've done:

1. I started off easy. I gave it access to the download folder on my Mac. 71 files, 30GB. It took a look through and told me what I should delete, what I might want to delete, and how it would sort the rest. In a few minutes, it was all done. A job I've been putting off forever, fixed.

2. I built a marketing outreach tracker in Notion for the launch of my book (coming May 12th!). I had various messy bits of open text and tables in different places: Notes, Notion, Gmail, and Google Sheets. Over an afternoon, Claude and I pulled it together and made a solid tracker with folks I could reach out to and offer a copy of the book. It helped me research potential contacts and pull together notes on their public conversations about parenting. This would have taken days of work, but was done in a few hours.

3. I (or, by this point, I feel like I should be saying "we") built an advertising tracker for trying to land a brand partnership for the new season of The New Fatherhood podcast. I needed a centralised system I can use with my managing producer. We are already talking to some potential advertisers, and want to reach out to more. It suggested 30 brands of the size and brand fit to treat as the first wave of outreach, gave me a first-pass draft on the email I can send (based on a few emails I've spent time crafting over the last few months) and I'll spend some time editing before sending them out this week.

4. We built and deployed a progressive web app to help dads understand if they may be dealing with paternal post-natal depression. I vibecoded a prototype with Lovable last year, but was never happy with it. Working with Claude, I rewrote the app, deployed it to GitHub and got it running with Netlify. It's prettier, better and cheaper than it was a week ago.

I did this on top of the regular load of meetings, school pickups and yet another email telling me there's a lice outbreak in my kid's class (NOOOOOOOOO!).

In a previous life (for a short time after leaving University), I wrote code for a living. It was some of the worst code you'd ever see. I quickly realised I was more curious about "why" someone would push a button than about "how" things happened after they did. And that led me into a world of strategy, leaving code behind for those more capable than I ever could be.

In the years since, I've longed for the ability to easily put together small pieces of software to solve repeatable problems I had—a way to expand my ability to do what time, attention, and cost could not.

That day is here. Thanks, Anthropic.
