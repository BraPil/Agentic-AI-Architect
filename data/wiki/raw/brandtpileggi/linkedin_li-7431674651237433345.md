I taught my AI agents to rewrite their own ops manual. Not vibes.A Genetic-Pareto feedback loop.

My stack: `GEPA optimize_anything` → Claude Code → pass/fail/time/logs → improved skill → repeat.

The trick is I treat the agent's SKILL as a tunable parameter. Real tasks become the evaluator. Logs become "Actionable Side Info”, a fancy term for what actually broke. The agent writes its own post-mortem, then `optimize_anything` proposes a sharper skill. Keep what moves the metric. Iterate.

Now I type: `/oa, make my agent better at this 20-year-old codebase`
It doesn't get more confident. It gets more correct.

Self-improving agents. Offline. Local. 
The AI factory is building itself🔧

Make sure you own your AI. AI in the cloud is not aligned with you; it’s aligned with the company that owns it.
