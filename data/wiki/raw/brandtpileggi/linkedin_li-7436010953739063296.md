Tired of Claude forgetting your repo conventions the second you start a new session? 😩
You drop a CLAUDE.md... and it still suggests outdated patterns, ignores your auth rules, or forgets where anything lives.
Here's the real fix: CLAUDE.md alone isn't enough. To make Claude act like a senior engineer who's lived in your codebase for years, give it these 4 permanent pieces:
1️⃣ CLAUDE.md — the always-on memory
Keep it lean: just WHY (project purpose), WHAT (high-level map/structure), HOW (rules, commands, conventions). If it's a wall of text, Claude tunes out the good stuff.
2️⃣ .claude/skills/ — reusable expert behaviors
Stop copy-pasting the same instructions. Turn code review checklists, refactor templates, debugging flows, release steps into skills. Claude loads them consistently across sessions and teammates.
3️⃣ .claude/hooks/ — unbreakable guardrails
Models forget. Hooks don't. Auto-run formatters after edits, block changes to auth/billing folders, enforce tests on critical paths. Automation that fires every time, no exceptions.
4️⃣ docs/ + local CLAUDE.md files — smart context loading
Don't bloat the main file. Point to architecture docs, ADRs, runbooks. Add tiny CLAUDE.md files in risky folders (e.g. src/auth/CLAUDE.md) . Claude picks up the gotchas exactly when it needs them.
Prompt hacks are temporary. This structure is permanent.
