Anthropic quietly showed one of the smartest ways to make Claude better over time.

It is not a new model.
It is one markdown file.

Boris Cherny, the creator of Claude Code, shared part of how their team works internally:

They use a CLAUDE.md file.
You write your rules once.
 Claude reads them before every session.
 Your workflow becomes consistent.
 Your corrections start compounding.

Here’s the setup:

1. For Claude Code
Create a file called CLAUDE.md
 Put it inside your project folder.
Claude uses it as persistent context for how you want work done.
That means you can tell it things like:
✦ Always plan before writing code
 ✦ For tasks with multiple steps, write a short spec first
 ✦ Run tests before marking anything complete
 ✦ Check logs before claiming a fix works
 ✦ Avoid quick hacks when a cleaner solution exists
 ✦ Document repeated mistakes so they do not happen again

This is where it gets powerful:
 Week 1: you correct Claude a few times
 Week 4: it starts following your workflow naturally
 Week 12: it feels less like a tool and more like a real teammate

2. For your personal workflow
You can do something similar with a profile.md file.
Use it to store:
✦ Your name, role, and priorities
 ✦ Your tone and writing preferences
 ✦ Your preferred output formats
 ✦ Things the AI should avoid
 ✦ Your active projects and current context

Instead of repeating yourself in every session,
 you give the AI a permanent operating manual.

That is the real lesson here:
The best AI users are not just prompting better.
They are building systems around the model.
One file will not make Claude magical overnight.

But one good context file, improved over time, can completely change how useful it becomes.

That is how AI stops feeling random
 and starts feeling operational.
