Massachusetts Institute of Technology just made its entire AI/ML book library free.

Twelve textbooks. The actual foundations. Zero paywall.

And most engineers who save this post will never open one of them.

I've watched this play out for years. Free was never the thing standing between you and understanding deep learning. Finishing was.

The honest math on a list like this:
→ You'll bookmark all 12
→ You'll open maybe 2
→ You'll finish 0

Not because the books are hard. Because "I'll read it later" is where free resources go to die. A $200 course you paid for gets finished. A free PDF gets a tab that stays open for six months.

So here's how I'd actually use these, if I were starting over:

1️⃣ Pick ONE based on the gap you have right now, not the one that looks most impressive.
2️⃣ Read 10 pages. Implement one idea from those 10 pages in code today
3️⃣ Only then earn the next book

The list everyone's sharing (full thing in comments):
Foundations of ML · Understanding Deep Learning · the Goodfellow Deep Learning · Sutton & Barto RL · Probabilistic ML · Fairness and ML · and 6 more.

All world-class. All free. All useless if they stay in a tab.
