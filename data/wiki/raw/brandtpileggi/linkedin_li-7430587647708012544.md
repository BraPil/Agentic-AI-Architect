I tapped into my agents' chatter today. Not because it was broken. Because it was too quiet.

10 workers, synthetic engineering tasks, telemetry streaming, the whole orchestra. No cloud. No UI. Just a terminal and the sound of tokens being manufactured.

- Model: Qwen3 Coder Next
- Target QPS: 12
- Prompt TPS: ~333 | Output TPS: ~319
- Latency P95: ~110ms
- Error rate: 0.0%
- Hardware: ZGX, a GPU the size of my palm

What I saw looked less like logs, more like a distributed company at work

→ Worker 01: writing incident runbooks 
→ Worker 04: comparing serving strategies diagnosing KV-cache pressure 
→ Worker 08: optimizing token-shape throughput 
→ Worker 10: already drafting the engineering memo before anyone asked

The control plane (me) just watching. Sipping coffee. The terminal now looks like a NOC for a software team made entirely of tokens.

Three things hit different:
1. Multi-agent doesn't feel like "one model faster”, it feels like parallel thinking
2. Bottleneck isn't generation speed anymore. Its coordination and scheduling
3. Live telemetry changes the mindset: this isn't inference. This is operations

Make sure you own your AI. AI in the cloud is not aligned with you; it’s aligned with the company that owns it.
