All legacy codebase modernization efforts in 2026 begin with: “let’s give an agent the repos”
Which is a great way to let Claude Code, opencode, Copilot, and friends misunderstand 40 repositories at machine speed.

In real enterprise code estates, the architecture usually lives in:
one staff engineer’s head… who retired in 2024,
three geological eras of DI,
`HttpClient` chains crossing repo boundaries,
and EF Core mappings to tables named by ancient forces.

My agents use local AI with dede, an engineering R&D context system, short for `DogEatDog.DependencyExplorer`

It scans a workspace full of sibling repos and extracts a deterministic Roslyn-backed AST/semantic graph across controllers, services, methods, internal HTTP calls, EF Core lineage, and cross-repo dependencies.

Here is a live scan over 13 repos, 1,962 projects, and 52,800 methods.
Not regex theater. 
Not architecture fan fiction. 
Not LLM vibes.
Just 100% local AI and tooling.

The useful part is giving that graph thingy to AI coding agents as a skill.
Now agents can query compiler-backed structure instead of improvising:
“What breaks if I change this endpoint?” 
“Which tables are in the blast radius?” 
“Where does this service cross repo boundaries?” 
“Which edges are ambiguous and need a human?”

Make sure you own your AI. AI in the cloud is not aligned with you; it’s aligned with the company that owns it.
