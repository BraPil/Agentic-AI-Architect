Notes on AI and inequality.

I think a real concern is how much AI development is concentrated and controlled by a handful of companies in just a few countries.

This means the institutional dynamics of those places, mainly the US and China, won't just influence innovation but also shapes who gets access on what terms and what interest this technology serves.

So rather than the simple question of which countries have AI, the better question is which institutional environments allow AI's benefits to diffuse broadly versus being captured by narrow interests.

We can already see how AI naturally rewards capital and complements high-leverage workers unless counterbalanced. And much of the current AI deployment is capital intensive (compute, cloud, proprietary data, integration) and this tends to raise the bargaining power and pay of workers who direct AI systems while increasing returns to owners of capital and IP.

The international monetary fund summarizes this risk directly.

"AI can increase labor income equality if it complements higher income workers while higher capital return while higher capital returns can raise the wealth inequality."

If we look at other technologies that have shaped our political and economic environment, something like the Industrial Revolution took generations to become clear...we're likely in a similar moment of uncertainty now, only our time to adjust doesn't feel like generations.

What steps should be taking now to level set?
